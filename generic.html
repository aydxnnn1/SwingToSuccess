Rules

1. Play the Ball as It Lies: Players must play the ball from where it comes to rest after a stroke.

2. Teeing Ground: The ball must be teed up within the designated teeing area.

3. Ball in Motion: A player must not make a stroke while another player’s ball is in motion.

4. Lost Ball or Out of Bounds: If a ball is lost or out of bounds, the player must play another ball from where the previous stroke was made with a penalty of one stroke.

5. Unplayable Ball: A player may declare their ball unplayable at any place on the course except in a water hazard. The player must then proceed under penalty of one stroke.

6. Substituting Ball: The player must hole out with the ball played from the teeing area, except when the Rules allow the player to substitute another ball.

7. Ball Holed: A ball is considered holed when it is at rest within the circumference of the hole and all of it is below the level of the lip.

8. Advice: Players must not give or seek advice during a round, except from their caddie.

9. Course Care: Players must take care not to damage the course. This includes repairing divots, ball marks, and raking bunkers.

10. Relief from Abnormal Ground Conditions: A player may take relief without penalty from certain abnormal ground conditions, including ground under repair, casual water, and animal holes.

11. Wrong Ball: If a player plays a wrong ball, they must correct the mistake by playing the correct ball or proceeding under the Rules. There is a penalty for playing a wrong ball.

12. Starting Time: Players must start at the time established by the Committee. Starting late can result in penalties.

13. Ball Deflected by Outside Agency: If a player’s ball in motion after a stroke is accidentally deflected or stopped by any outside agency, it is a rub of the green, and the ball must be played as it lies.

14. Movable Obstruction: A player may take relief from interference by a movable obstruction, such as a bench or a ball marker, without penalty.

15. Cleaning Ball on Green: A player may lift and clean their ball on the putting green. The ball must be replaced on its original spot.

16. Provisional Ball: If a player believes their ball is lost outside a water hazard or out of bounds, they may play a provisional ball from the spot of the previous stroke.

17. Flagstick: The flagstick may be attended, removed, or held up before a player makes a stroke from anywhere on the course.

18. Ball Moved After Address: If a ball moves after it has been addressed, the player incurs a one-stroke penalty and must replace the ball.

19. Line of Putt: Players must not touch the line of their putt except to remove loose impediments or repair ball marks and old hole plugs.

20. Failure to Hole Out: In stroke play, if a competitor fails to hole out on any hole, they are disqualified.

Terminology

1. Par: The number of strokes a skilled golfer is expected to need to complete a hole.

2. Birdie: A score of one stroke under par on a hole.

3. Bogey: A score of one stroke over par on a hole.

4. Eagle: A score of two strokes under par on a hole.

5. Ace: Also known as a hole-in-one, this is when a player gets the ball into the hole with a single stroke from the tee.

6. Handicap: A system used to level the playing field by allowing less skilled players to compete fairly against more skilled players.

7. Fore: A warning shout to alert other players or spectators of a potentially errant golf ball.

8. Green: The area around the hole where the grass is cut very short.

9. Fairway: The area between the tee box and the green, where the grass is cut short.

10. Rough: Areas outside of the fairway with longer grass, making shots more difficult.

11. Bunker: A hazard filled with sand; players must not ground their club before striking.

12. Water Hazard: Any body of water that poses a challenge; penalties apply if the ball lands in a hazard.

13. Stroke Play: The total number of strokes taken over the course of the round determines the winner.

14. Match Play: Players compete hole-by-hole, with the player winning the most holes declared the winner.

15. Provisional Ball: A second ball played when the first ball may be lost or out of bounds.

16. Unplayable Lie: A situation where the ball cannot be played; a penalty stroke is incurred.

17. Relief: A player may take relief from certain conditions without penalty, such as ground under repair.

18. Obstructions: Artificial objects on the course from which players are entitled to relief.

19. Tee: A small device used to elevate the ball for the initial stroke on each hole.

20. Mulligan: A do-over shot not counted against the score, often allowed in informal play.

Etiquette

Respect Other Players: Always show respect to fellow golfers by being quiet and still while they are taking their shots.

Maintain Pace of Play: Keep up with the pace of play to avoid holding up other groups. Be ready to play when it is your turn.

Care for the Course: Repair divots, rake bunkers, and fix ball marks on the green. Leave the course in good condition for other players.

Proper Attire: Wear appropriate golf attire, which typically includes collared shirts and golf shoes.

Safety: Always be aware of your surroundings to ensure that you do not endanger others with your shots or actions.

Honesty and Integrity: Golf is a game of honor, and players are expected to be honest in keeping score and adhering to the rules.

Techniques

Driving: Using a driver to hit the ball as far as possible down the fairway. Key tips include ensuring a proper grip, maintaining a relaxed stance, and swinging smoothly.

Putting: The act of hitting the ball with a putter to roll it into the hole. Key tips include reading the green, using a pendulum motion, and hitting the ball squarely.

Chipping: A short shot intended to get the ball onto the green and close to the hole. Key tips include using a lofted club, keeping your weight on your front foot, and following through.

Iron Play: Using irons to hit the ball from the fairway or rough. Key tips include choosing the right iron, making clean contact, and maintaining a steady tempo.

Bunker Shots: Open the clubface, aim to hit the sand just behind the ball, and follow through to ensure the ball gets out of the bunker.

Draw: A shot that curves slightly from right to left (for right-handed golfers). Achieved by adjusting grip and swing path.

Fade: A shot that curves slightly from left to right (for right-handed golfers). Achieved by opening the stance and adjusting swing path.

Flop Shot: A high, soft shot played with a lofted club, typically used to get over an obstacle or stop quickly on the green. Key tips include opening the clubface and using a steep swing.
